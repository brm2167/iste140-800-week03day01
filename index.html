Bold, italics, <strong>, and <em>

Don't use <b> and <i>, these are deprecated, HTML should be used for structure, not design.

You can use <strong> and <em>, as by default these are bold or italic, but they don't have to be.

HTML Entities

  • &copy; ©
  • &lt; <
  • &gt; >
  • &amp; &
  • &nbsp; " " (Non-breaking space)

There are many other symbols, as well as emojis, that can be obtained with HTML entities.

Structural Elements

The <div> tag is used for block elements, and the <span> tag is used for inline elements.

More Structural Elements

  • <header>Document headings go here </header>
  • <nav>Navigation goes here</nav>
  • <main>Content goes here</main>
  • <footer>Document footer goes here</footer>

HTML elements describe content meaning and structure, not the appearance.

Why Follow Standards?

  • More consistent display across platforms
  • "Forward Compatibility" with future browsers & devices
  • HTML can render differently in different browsers
  • Adhering to standards does not guarantee that HTML pages will display consistently between browsers, but it will certainly increase the odds that they will
